Company Formation in Dubai

Mainland

The UAE Mainland comprises businesses licensed by the Department of Economic Development (DED) in each of the seven emirates: Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Sharjah, Ajman, Fujairah, Ras Al Khaimah, and Umm Al Quwain. Mainland companies have no restrictions on trade within the UAE, allowing them to operate locally and internationally. Dubai Mainland, in particular, is a popular choice due to its strategic location, advanced infrastructure, and access to a diverse business ecosystem. Mainland businesses also allow 100% foreign ownership in most sectors, making it a highly attractive option for entrepreneurs.

Freezone (9 Major Freezones in the UAE)

Freezones are specialized economic areas offering 100% foreign ownership, tax benefits, and customized business regulations. Below are some key Freezones in the UAE:

  • Dubai Multi Commodities Centre (DMCC) – Best for trade, commodities, and financial services.
  • Dubai Silicon Oasis (DSO) – Ideal for tech startups and IT businesses.
  • Jebel Ali Free Zone (JAFZA) – A hub for industrial, logistics, and export businesses.
  • Abu Dhabi Global Market (ADGM) – A financial Freezone focusing on banking and fintech.
  • Sharjah Media City (SHAMS) – Suitable for media, entertainment, and creative businesses.
  • Fujairah Free Zone (FFZ) – Best for industrial and shipping businesses due to its port access.
  • Ras Al Khaimah Economic Zone (RAKEZ) – A cost-effective zone for startups and manufacturing.
  • Ajman Free Zone (AFZ) – A growing hub for SMEs and e-commerce businesses.
  • Umm Al Quwain Free Trade Zone (UAQ FTZ) – Best for startups looking for affordable licensing options.

Offshore

Offshore companies in the UAE are set up for international trade, asset protection, and tax benefits. These companies cannot conduct business within the UAE but are ideal for holding assets, opening bank accounts, and global trading. Offshore entities, such as those in Jebel Ali (JAFZA), Ras Al Khaimah (RAKICC), and Ajman Offshore, offer confidentiality, low operational costs, and ease of setup, but they hold less priority compared to Mainland and Freezone businesses due to their limited operational scope.
